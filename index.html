Cripta de la Catedral de la Almudena 12:30h

Below the Almudena Cathedral lies this beautiful Romanesque Revival church with a Latin-cross plan. It features over 400 columns, each crowned by a different capital adorned with biblical figures, nature scenes and even the symbol of Madrid, the bear and the strawberry tree.
It has the same dimensions as the cathedral and is considered to be the largest crypt in the country. The entrance, which is located in front of the Arab Wall, is made up of three semi-circular arches. The resting place of some of Spain’s most noble families, and to King Alfonso XII's first wife María de las Mercedes de Orléans, the crypt has 20 chapels, each with its own beautiful stained-glass window, as well as a number of ornate artworks. Particularly noteworthy is the painting of Our Lady of the Lily, possibly the oldest image of the Virgin in the city.

La Masía de José Luis after ceremony

A masia is a type of rural construction common to all the old Crown of Aragon: Catalonia, Valencian Community, Aragon, Languedoc and Provence (in the south of France). Through the ages, the materials used to construct masies varied, often determined by their location. In mountainous areas, rough stone was often used, except for doorways, windows and arches, where stone was worked. During the Middle Ages, mud was used as mortar, though later on it was replaced by quicklime or cement. In places where stone was hard to come by, adobe was more common as a construction material.

La Masía de José Luis was reformed and inaugurated in 2003 thanks to Pascua Ortega who has designed all the rooms. La Masía has a cozy Mediterranean-style where you can enjoy in a pleasant and relaxed atmosphere.
